Charles Babbage (1791-1871)

Timeline

1791

Charles Babbage was born in London, England.

1810-1814

He went to Cambridge for College.
Specifically Trinity and Peterhouse where he would graduate there.
While he was there, he teamed up people like John Herschel and George Peacock.
He made a small calculator that can perform certain math equation up to eight decimals.

1823

He gained government support to make the "Difference Engine".
It had a decimal capacity up to 20 decimals and also does calculations like the small calculator that he had.
It was sort of like a computer. It had storage like a computer as well. Although it wasn't fully built.

Mid 1830s

Babbage made plans for the "Analytical Engine".
The forerunner for modern computers.
This was supposed to make automatic calculations from punched cards.

1843-1871

Ada Lovelace got translated a French paper and published how it could perform it's own calculations.
Although it was never made until a group a scientists made an accurate model according to the specs provided by Babbage in 1991. Babbage died in 1871.
